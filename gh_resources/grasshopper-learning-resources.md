# Learning Grasshopper: A Resource Guide

A curated path for students learning Grasshopper for Rhino. Work top to bottom: get comfortable with the interface, build a few small projects, then tackle data management before moving into the full deep-dive references.

---

## UI

Learn the canvas, components, and wiring before touching geometry.

- **The Grasshopper Primer (Third Edition) — Foundations chapter.** Covers the canvas, component anatomy, parameters, and wiring.
  https://modelab.gitbooks.io/grasshopper-primer → *1. Foundations*

- **David Rutten — Getting Started series, Lesson 01 "Interface Basics."** The canvas and your first definition, from Grasshopper's creator.
  https://vimeopro.com/rhino/grasshopper-getting-started-by-david-rutten

- **Hopific — Mastering the Grasshopper Interface.** Finding/adding components, reading the gray/orange/red health colors, referencing Rhino geometry, and dragging wires.
  https://hopific.com/the-grasshopper-interface-a-beginners-guide/

- **TU Delft Open Access — Lesson 1 "Basics."** Interface tour, canvas navigation, and the Number Slider.
  https://interactivetextbooks.tudelft.nl/rhino-grasshopper/Grasshopper_Rhino_course/

---

## First Steps

Quick-start projects to build something real.

- **Brendan Harmon — Introduction to Grasshopper.** Build points, lines, and a sine-wave curve, then continue into the **Parametric Bench** follow-on. A clean first project.
  https://baharmon.github.io/basics

- **Parametric House — beginner builds.** Short, self-contained projects (parametric vase, tower-on-a-curve, facade, contoured wall) for repetition once the UI clicks.
  https://parametrichouse.com/rhino-grasshopper/

- **TU Delft — Lesson 1 exercise.** The "Building shapes in Rhino and Grasshopper" exercise, with a full solution if you get stuck.

- **How to Rhino — beginner tutorials.** Project-based videos; filter for "beginner" titles.
  https://howtorhino.com/rhino-grasshopper-tutorials/

---

## Data Management

Lists and trees — the part most students find hardest.

- **TU Delft — Lesson 3 "Data structures."** Branches, lists, and items, plus flatten / graft / simplify, with exercises and solutions.
  https://interactivetextbooks.tudelft.nl/rhino-grasshopper/Grasshopper_Rhino_course/

- **The Grasshopper Primer — "What is a Data Tree?"** The list-of-lists mental model, paths as a folder structure, and the Param Viewer / Tree Statistics components.
  https://modelab.gitbooks.io/grasshopper-primer → *1.5*

- **ShapeDiver — Data Trees Explained (Pt. 1 & 2).** Items vs. lists vs. trees, item-wise behavior, and how data flow affects compute speed.
  https://www.shapediver.com/blog/grasshopper-data-trees-explained-pt-1

- **BIM Corner — Beginner's Guide to Data Trees.** Article plus companion video and downloadable exercises; strong on visual intuition.
  https://bimcorner.com/a-beginners-guide-to-data-trees-in-grasshopper/

- **ETH Zurich — Digital Design Methods: Data Tree.** Concise written reference with a worked graft/flatten example.
  https://ddm.arch.ethz.ch/docs/tutorial/grasshopper-basics/data-tree

---

## Deep Dives

Full guides to work through once the basics are solid.

- **The Grasshopper Primer (full).** The complete free reference — foundations through advanced data and geometry.
  https://modelab.gitbooks.io/grasshopper-primer

- **TU Delft Open Access course (full).** The complete structured lesson sequence with exercises.
  https://interactivetextbooks.tudelft.nl/rhino-grasshopper/Grasshopper_Rhino_course/

- **Coded Shapes — A Practical Guide to Data Trees.** Intermediate; how an experienced user structures, splits, and re-merges trees by path rather than just defining terms.
  https://www.codedshapes.com/p/a-practical-guide-to-data-trees-in

- **Rhino official learning hub.** McNeel's link-checked aggregator of Grasshopper material across all levels.
  https://www.rhino3d.com/learn/ → filter to Grasshopper
